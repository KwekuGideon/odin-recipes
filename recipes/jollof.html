Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a rice dish from West Africa. The dish is typically made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, onion, spices, vegetables and meat in a single pot, although its Ingredients and preparation methods vary across different regions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain rice
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic minced
  • 1 large red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 can of tomato paste(6 ounces)
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon od paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Optional: 1 cup of frozen green peas, 1 red chili pepper(such as scotch bonnets), diced, and 1 pound of chicken or shrimp.

Note: The ingredients and seasonings may vary based on personal taste and regional differences.

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice and set it aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Add onion, garlic, and red bell pepper to the pot and cook until the onion is soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, thyme, basil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add the broth and bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Stir in the rice, making sure it is evenly distributed in the pot.
  7. If desired, add in the frozen green peas and diced chili pepper.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice cook for 18-20 minutes, or until the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is cooked enough.
  9. If using, add in the chicken or shrimp and cook for additional 5-7 minutes, or until the meat is fully cooked.
  10. Serve hot.

Enjoy your delicious Jollof Rice!
